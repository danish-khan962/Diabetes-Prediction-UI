Predict Your Risk of Diabetes Today

Diabetes is a serious condition that affects millions worldwide. Early detection can help you take control of your health before complications arise. By taking a simple risk assessment, you can gain valuable insights into your likelihood of developing diabetes. The good news is that with early intervention, lifestyle changes, and proper management, you can reduce your risk and improve your overall well-being. Take the first step today and find out where you stand.

leafy greens

Leafy greens are not only low in carbs but also high in essential nutrients like vitamins A, C, and K. These vegetables help regulate blood sugar by providing fiber, which slows down sugar absorption, making them an excellent choice for diabetes management.

berries

Berries, including strawberries, raspberries, and blueberries, are rich in antioxidants, which help reduce oxidative stress. They’re also lower in sugar compared to other fruits and have a low glycemic index, helping to keep blood sugar levels stable.

nuts

Nuts like almonds and walnuts are excellent sources of healthy fats, fiber, and protein. They help slow down the digestion of carbohydrates, which can prevent sudden spikes in blood sugar. They are also good for heart health, a concern for people with diabetes.

sweet potatoes

Sweet potatoes are a fiber-rich, complex carbohydrate that provides slow-releasing energy. With a lower glycemic index than regular potatoes, they help maintain steady blood sugar levels, making them a better choice for diabetes.

Oats

Oats, a whole grain rich in soluble fiber like beta-glucan, help improve insulin sensitivity and lower blood sugar. Steel-cut or rolled oats slow carbohydrate absorption, keeping blood sugar levels stable, making them a great breakfast for those with diabetes.

Chia seeds

Chia seeds are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and protein. They help regulate blood sugar, promote fullness, and aid in digestion, making them ideal for managing diabetes and weight. Their antioxidants also support overall health and wellness.

cinnamon

Cinnamon is a potent spice that improves insulin sensitivity and regulates blood sugar. It contains compounds that mimic insulin’s effects and lower blood sugar. Adding cinnamon to meals or drinks enhances flavor while supporting blood glucose control.

garlic

Garlic is known for its health benefits, including improving blood sugar regulation. It contains allicin, which boosts insulin production and sensitivity. Adding garlic to meals enhances flavor while supporting metabolic health and better blood sugar management.
